SPARKING SMILES: NURTURING DENTAL HEALTH

Why is Dental health important in early childhood?

Health is integral to our daily functioning and shapes our identity, as noted by Chris Wilson. Children aged 3 to 6 are susceptible to cavities due to poor hygiene and unhealthy diets, necessitating parental support in instilling good oral practices. While these children begin to assert independence, parental supervision remains vital in establishing effective oral hygiene routines that will impact their long-term well-being. Encouraging nutritious eating, limiting sugary foods, ensuring regular brushing and flossing, using mouthwash, and promoting good sleep habits are essential for fostering stronger and healthier development.

Cognitive

To identify basic parts of the mouth.
To understand the importance of brushing the teeth 3x a day.
To distinguish between food that are good and bad for their health.

Affective


To recognize any concerns regarding dental health care and encourage regular visits to dental clinic.

The Proper Way to Brush Your Teeth


Step 1: Start with the outer surfaces of your teeth. Take your time!

Step 2: Tilt your brush at a 45° angle.

Step 3: Brush the inner surface of your teeth.

Step 4: Clean the chewing surfaces of your teeth.

Step 5: Brush your tongue.

Eat Healthy Foods

A healthy diet is essential for good health and nutrition.
It protects you against many chronic non-communicable diseases, such as heart disease, diabetes, and cancer. Eating a variety of foods and consuming less salt, sugars, and saturated and industrially-produced trans-fats, are essential for a healthy diet.

Visit Your Dentist

Dentists not only solve problems, they can prevent them.
Potential problems they can spot include the onset of gum disease and dental decay.
When it comes to your oral health it is important you are proactive and seeing your dentist regularly so that they can catch any issues early, before they become a problem.
